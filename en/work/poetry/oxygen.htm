Oxygen

The cold chill on my fingers
Reminds me that I am alive, still,
Somehow, in this asphalt world
I still think and feel just as vividly

As when I was out in the forest,
Speckled now with dead trees dotting
The landscape with little grey spots,
But the shape still remains

Of a living, breathing entity
And I, too, breathe out
The blood flowing through my veins
Taking in that sweet, precious, oxygen

The essential air of life.
