Changing Blue

The days are getting shorter now,
The cerulean blue afternoons where I sat
Outside resplendent in the light
Of the day replaced

By the looming silvery shadow of the evening
Faint salmon pink glow growing weaker
As it fails to overcome the deepening maw
The blue I see all around

A flat featureless colour
Like a tundra without people
Around to share the warmth, or even

A few reindeer and some silly bells,
The fun is gone
And Christmas is too
Far away, again.
