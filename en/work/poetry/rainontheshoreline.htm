Rain on the Shoreline

The belting rain on the shoreline
Melts into sand little craters
That break the monotony
Of so many evenings
Battered into oblivion
By unyielding politeness.

A new life emerges
In the scattered shapes
Left behind
As the tide withdraws
And leaves in its place
The gleaming portrait
Of another world.

Porcelain white it pierces
The glittering sky and draws
A pair of errant eyes down
To the lonely light lingering
On the horizon.

This way has been walked before.

The broken waves churn
The sand anew
As the rain refreshes
Its soft tableau
And paints again its picture
Of another world:

A breathtaking view
