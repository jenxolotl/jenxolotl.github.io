Bracelet

Sitting next to you
On the early morning train
Dead tired blue collar man
Face pressed firmly against
The pole between us
Hands gripping like the world
Will fall if you let go

I see the bracelet
The pink white and blue
Beads in the shadow of your cuff
Against your skin slightly weathered
For someone our age, hair holding
A faded remnant of green dye
Tussled mullet all the rage

Cheek pressing deeper into unyielding
Metal, supporting, unflinching hi vis strip
On your calf glowing dimly in the dawn
Light me in unironed black officewear
I wonder

Where you are going
If your night was alright
If you are happy
If you have someone
Who asks you these questions
If anyone else
Has seen you

I want to compliment the bracelet
But it slips from view too fast
Back into the darkness, the tired
Stares and little screens in this
Microcosm of society, I feel
Self-conscious, a statistical
Anomaly

Did you notice me too?
Not wearing the colours, but perhaps
Obvious enough drawn into the lines
Of my face, you’re just an
Ordinary bloke
You check your phone and bound
With sudden energy for the exit

With clinking metal workboots, disappearing
Into the crowd at Cockburn Central, people
Pouring past the window and through the door
As I engross myself in a book and
Wait for my own stop to come.
