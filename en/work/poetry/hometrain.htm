Home Train

Long ride home
In the old sardine tube
The mashed pastiche of
Familiar faces slowly thinning out

As the chipped bricks
With faded red paint
Crumble and give way to no man’s land,
The indeterminate space

Between city and suburb
Where bleak cardboard houses coexist
With the tall steel sheds
And dire fields of scrap,

Jagged metal twisting
And poking holes in
The cleared expanses
Of earth,

The transmigrated scars
Of progress,
The glittering centre stage
And home beyond

In the pale yellow glow
Of a setting Sun,
Sand falling in the sea,
By the limestone cliffs

With warning signs:
A place to spend another night.
