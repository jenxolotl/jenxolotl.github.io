Without You

How did I not see the signs
The strain
The incredible pain
You must've felt deep inside.

I found you
Still hiding from the world
And took your hand —
Watching you grow and thrive
One incredible man.

It was always within you
The charm, the confidence
The style and gregarious smiles
That made you who you were.

It only took someone to see
For the world to bathe in your glorious light.

And yet perhaps I briefly lost sight
If only for a moment —
Of the man inside.

I blinked and
You were gone.

And now I stare
At a world that has lost its edges
Without definition
Blank signs and colourless hedges

I would have listened,
But in my blindness didn't ask
The question that could change everything.

I would have listened, but I didn't see.

The grasses and the trees
Whisper their hushed cries
And I am without
As I am within.

Cold like the snow
On that winter day.

I would stand with you again
But there is no ledge
And only nothing lies beyond

The quiet hush is deafening

Without your voice to drown it out
