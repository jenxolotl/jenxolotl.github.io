Hyde Park in Summer

In thrall of the Moreton fig trees
Ringing the lake, neatly topping out
Against the blue monochrome sky

The warmth of the sun still
Striking the skin on my leg
Filtered through the swatches

Of shimmering green leaves
Still rustling, still blowing on
The cyclical concrete path

Heating up the vestigial slick
Of sunscreen yet to fade away,
The waterbirds the couples

Preening in their twos
The children playing badminton
In cosy grassy clearings

Against the gentle breeze,
The perfectly tuned temperature
To unwind, undress, relax

A man sits intently reading
A red book with gold letters
Framed against the lake

He strides past with it in hand
And I squint to know the title
But can’t quite make it out
