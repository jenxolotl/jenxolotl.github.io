Boyagin Rock

Sitting by Boyagin Rock in the shade of the blotch barked wandoo trees,
Surrounded by the peaceful solitude of trilling insects and chirping birds
With the woodsmoke scent of a cup of tea filling my nose, same as it did

Yesterday, up on the hill overlooking everything, the long grey crochet lines and mosaic shapes
Of an entire world below, the green ferries crossing paths and silver train rolling past
Under the wispy cirrus clouds like an old-time Hollywood panorama, your style sitting timeless

On the lawn, before the fields of flowers; lime green banksias, kangaroo paws, tiny pink ones
Whose name I can’t remember, the feeling of your arm in my hand, the softness of your skin beneath the leather,
The warmth of the sun on my legs, the burn of our eyes in the neon lights, living in the moment,

At this time, beside the sacred signs of the Waugyl, with life continuing all around, an ancient monolith sitting still
Beneath the gemlike Wheatbelt sky, despite all these birds and bugs around, the only butterflies I feel
Lie within me.
