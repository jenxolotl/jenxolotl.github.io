Potala of the Point

There is a place that I call heaven
Right outside my door;
Down the road at Peron,
Where the Great Indian meets the shore.

And the blisters that rise up
on the soles of my feet
become fellow pilgrims on the ascent

To the Promised Land.
It was called Mooriburdup, though
Everyone seems to have forgotten

That the feeling still remains
strong as ever, when the obs post
is sighted like a castle on a hill

Present but unable to impose
but its own smallness
on this majestic landscape,

The seascape where I rose
from the deep blue waters
To take my first breath

And it is as fresh as ever
In the salty spray where
like a Tibetan I might prostrate

To pray, at the Potala of the Point
A palace the envy
of Poseidon himself.

And it is no off the shelf feeling
To be there, in that breeze
A step away from the revving engines

And polluted streams
of Rockingham,
A figment of our minds

Made mellifluous at this Point,
Here, Now,
Where the sight

Of waves breaking
against unseen rocks
and wind singing

In limestone cliffs
Fills my eyes
With the Glory

Of a setting Sun,

On its way to light a fire
for the land we all came from,
In my heart, beating strong

The Great Indian touches my
blistered feet, and warms me
where it meets the shore.
