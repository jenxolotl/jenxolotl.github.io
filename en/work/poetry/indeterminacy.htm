Indeterminacy

WHERE'S THE MAN

I was speaking to on the phone?

The sound roars through reception's subdued modernity,

Each vibration shirt fronting the cheap plastic civility

that hides

The dark primæval feeling that unites

Animal and man

Where is he?,
the bellow resumes

his attempt to restore strict order to the shameless

facelessness before him; the source
of all his complaints.

I was talking to a man on the phone!

His steady eyes scan the ant farm office,
looking for the filthy orifice

This human termite has crawled back into

A dense nest
of hallways and cubicles seem
destined to avoid responsibility.

His eyes,
white with the rage of a falling star

Resentful of being ejected from the round order of
the cosmos

Find their square peg in mine, bright
like the eyes of a doe
straddled with snow

Facing the wrath of a ten-tonne truck.

Ah, ————

But they are not a man's eyes

And the urge to kill

Is it a masculine one? Read

The case of Zhu Ling

Finds its frustration, and blunting

In the face of a place where
Nobody names
the vague flower shapes

On the walls

Is it a dandelion?

Is she a man?

And primal feelings
are compressed
into standards
compliant boxes.

I could have alleviated
the man's complaint

But do not get paid enough

To receive the screams of a dying universe, and

Resolve indeterminate states.
