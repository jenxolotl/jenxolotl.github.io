Eating Figs

Every time I eat a fig, I think of you
Biting into your sinews, soft and sweet
In the moment they are ripe

The cold wind rends the branches bare
Yet the season will soon come again
Where they may be plucked from the tree

Plump in your hands, fresh on my tongue
The taste as strong as it always was
The hills of Bamiyan on my horizons

With a single line
You subsume my whole body
You, the thoughts behind my words
