Losar in the Heat

The snow capped mountains
And grey blue waters
Are far away, here

The rich mud on the paths
That wants to slough away under
The slightest footstep

The familiar faces in little towns
And yaks in bliss in endless fields of green
Are all far away, here

This place too is a kind of desert
But here it burns instead of chills
And the warmth of family is no solace

When they're all so far away.

Across the salty sea
Up the life giving rivers
In the mountains, the fortresses of

Home.

But in this desert of lizards and searing heat
Are there not also little towns of familiar faces
And cattle napping in endless fields of gold

The dusty pebbles give way under gentle step
On paths through hills not a tenth as mighty
But with spirits of their own

The way is universal through the changes
And, perhaps, here too
Could be home.
