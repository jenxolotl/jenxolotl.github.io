Procession

Into the bitter cold of darkness
A parade of powdered lights
Like a funeral procession

Moves solemnly
Down winding roads
To the same destination

The glass paned graveyards
And dust filled crematoria
Of youthful imagination

It is in their eyes
And the pock marks
On their face

The lights stop
And change colour to match
The dour red of

Disappointed hopes and dreams
Caught in glimpses
On hi-vis vests and

The stream of metal boxes
Flowing too tight
To breathe.

It will all be over soon.

The stone slabs shimmer
In monumental brilliance
As cheap coffee covers

The strain
Behind photographic smiles

I see my name
And want to turn but
There is nowhere to move.
