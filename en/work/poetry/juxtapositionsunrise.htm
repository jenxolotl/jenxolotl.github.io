Juxtaposition, sunrise

The thin slats of light
That slowly take away
What is precious to me,
The darkness, receding

As my heart fills with dread
My blood pressure rises
My lungs fill with the
Polluted air of a waking city

On a weekday
In Rockingham.

The sweeping gauze of light
That slowly filters through
The trees, giving sight to
What is precious to me,

The enchanted forest
Come back to life,
My mind to the brim
With fresh ideas,

My eyes wide open,
Feet ready to wander
Down the dirt track

On a weekend
In Serpentine.
