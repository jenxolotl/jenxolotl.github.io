Weekday

In the morning I say
My eyes are starting to feel heavy
And by the middle of the day

The feeling hasn’t gone though
I have a sandwich
The hunger distracting me

And the slightly salty taste of prosciutto
To keep me going a few hours longer
As the weight of the sun sinks

And presses the button into a train door
Grinding the masses into a halt
On the Swan River by the old mill

By the old brewery the old drunks
The slippery old fucks too few left to give
To the baby cooing on the blanket

With Mum smiling at her at me
Somehow smiling too
Eyes still heavy

Shoes sinking into soft sand
Soul somewhere over the hills
South Perth bloated like a swollen tongue

But it doesn’t hurt
