Warp Speed Through the Wheatbelt

This thing can go the speed of light:
Look how she purrs
In this vast expanse,

A tiger roaming
In stalks of wheat,

Skin branded by the Sun
That glows a glorious gold
As far as the eye can see

With nothing to stop us
In the bright open air
We lift up to meet the stars

Going warp speed through the Wheatbelt —
What would Janeway say
About this Delta Quadrant of ours:

A planet like no other
With us the dashing explorers
On our light speed ship.

One slip and it could be over,
A step too far, but the way here
Is clearer than any other

Gunbarrel straight,
Just keep to the open road.
Canals built by Martians

Who couldn't imagine
We would use them as waterslides
For our wide-eyed dreams

We take off fast, zooming
Past the realms of grounded earth
And safety, and logic

There aren't many places
Where you can't see Perth
But from here it looks

Pretty small, one dot
Among all the others
In the darkening sky.
