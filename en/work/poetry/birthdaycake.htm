Birthday Cake

One year ago, I walked with you
Down the brown dusty steps
Of a cosy Mexican Kitchen in Freo.

The warm interior almost matched
What I felt
With my arm, wrapped in soft fur,
Nested in your warm embrace.

The sky seemed to glow as I beamed
That the winter of endless rain
Was finally over.

The winter that drove us together,
On those dark and cold nights
Gave way to unveiled reality
As the light slowly returned.

You complained about the draught,
And the birthday cake that I had rushed
To you, an urgent symbol of my love
Gave you the shits.

It should not have surprised me
That everything soon melted away
Like frost in the morning sun.

One year on, I walk down the steps
Of Fremantle Cemetery,
Unsure what brings me there.

I wander through the plaques
And weathered headstones,
Looking at the endless names
And smiling faces

Of people now silent.

I find the signs of people I knew,
Thinking of the things we did,
The link between my eyes and a bit of
Bare metal, lying in the grass.

My denim jacket protects me
Against the lingering chill,
As myriad little flowers poke up
And colour the world anew.

I return to the safety of my car
And send my love
To those who matter most to me.

The warm spring is coming again,
The grey skies melting into
A sea of pink and yellow.
It all fades away, and

Not everything gets a plaque.
