Kalamunda

In the hills, by the feet of boulders
Along my path
Red dirt damp in the grey misty air
The city in my shadow

The world in my wake
Past the sun faded shop signs
Beyond the sitting Buddhas
Under thatched bath huts

Warm in my glow, the red hot sunset
Down the long narrow roads
Under the sheets in your bed
The stink of rum and coke

On your breath, the sounds
Broken records by the fire
Stains on the shag rug
Anime movies in the morning

And distant lights down below
The burr of engine brakes
The chill and cold dew
Alone by familiar lakes

The pain in my stomach
Sleepless tremors in my chest
Weary yellowed eyes
Stretched and pallid skin

Curious trinkets and paintings
In yards with gnomes and rusty mowers
Down the garden path to the bush
Trees tall and unrepentant

I walked
Without looking back
