Elbows

Whenever I shave my arms,
I always methodically start
First with the tapering lines
Of my lower arm, proceeding

Down to the wrist and repeating
Top and bottom, before going on
To the wide expanse of the upper region,
Clearing a path over the slightest suggestion

Of a bicep, a rounded and reduced
Form reminiscent of the geology of this land,
Not yet old and faded, and several days on
Notice that I’ve forgotten my elbows

The hairs rising together
Like windswept trees from the field,
The Dryandra to my barren Wheatbelt,
The liminal space between my logical concepts.

In my failure to achieve a perfect smoothing
I am embarrassed moreso, by the thought others
Might see me as a less than
Uniform object

But I must admit the unwanted tuft
Is still different, lingering, soft.
