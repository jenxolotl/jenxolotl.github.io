To a Pen

Little blue pen
Lying on the footpath
In the shadow of the bridgeway
Across RPH

I saw you there after work
Me, tired and weary
And you, sitting immaculately
Like an angel sitting over weathered bricks.

I thought of picking you up
And putting you to my own use
But thought of the Buddha's precept
To let you be as you were

I saw you again
Battered and bruised
The veins showing down your side,
And shattered plastic harshly piercing

The veil of tranquility.

Whether broken on the sidewalk
Or faded on the page
The striking colour still bleeds
From blue into grey.
