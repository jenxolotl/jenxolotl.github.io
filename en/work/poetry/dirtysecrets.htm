Dirty Secrets

Through the repetitions and daily motions
Of this post-industrial land, where the rusting sheds
And brick warehouses with faded paint
Are cut to pieces

By the tangled net of roads and broken
Sound barriers and square homes
Packed tight in grids like a dirty secret
From Nauru or Manus Island

To glimmer briefly caught in the morning Sun,
Monuments to be swallowed by
The twilight of our day, a looming decay
Of a forsaken age.

The same three people every morning
At the bus stop, effort repeating
Like a piston in the drive
To get closer to the heart of it all,

The beating centre across the river
Where we forget the smallness of our lives
For a moment, amidst the usual throng
Of doped up commuters

And get lost in the alleyways
Between shining glass towers.
