Sitting by the Brook (Gooralong Brook #1)

Out by the babbling brook in Jarrahdale
Surrounded by the staccato croaks and squeaks
Of a symphony of frogs, their voices bouncing off
The river’s edge like plastic ping pong balls

Off a wooden table tumbling into still flowing waters
At the start of spring, the current flowing into crests and troughs
Over a mazework of rocks, and silt covered logs

Sitting silent under the surface.

The Sun settles lower in the trees, descending
To the distant ocean as I write
By the water’s edge in the misty yellow rays
Thick with a spray of tiny gnats

In my adopted weekend home.
The wind gets colder now against my skin
And I think of all the photos I have sent
And words I have written, trying

To put to pixel and paper the thoughts behind my eyes.

I have showed you these scenes countless times
And hope you have seen my shadow on the bark
Somewhere, sharply delineated and swiftly fading

While the gum’s glimmering green sign on the water endures;
The frogs still croak and hum in the rustle of the wind
I look up from my obsession
And my face is gone.
