Coming Home in the Sunset

The sun outside my car window
After work, the anticipation
Of coming home from the toil
Shines through the plain

Of towering chimneys and pylons
Powering a dynamo that makes the sea
Of clouds look dainty
In its wake,

A beautiful industrial waste
Intersected by the tangled mess
Of wires that a bird could drown in
Closer to home now

And the sky turns a brilliant pale blue
Like the colour of your eyes
Behind the grain silo
The Sun a ball of light

Behind the palms
And strange flat canopies
Of exotic trees, silhouetted
Like some lost primordial paradise

I am home
And finally
The sky burns
A brilliant blazing pink
