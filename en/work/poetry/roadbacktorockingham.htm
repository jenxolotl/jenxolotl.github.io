The Road Back to Rockingham

Racing down the freeway as fast
As our laws will allow us —
The silhouette of the tall steel sheds
And blocks of cookie cutter homes

Turning black against the red
Of the cooling Sun setting
In the dusky summer sky,
Traffic filtering out
Until there is only road ahead;

The long grey line and street lamps lighting up
Mean that home is coming soon.
A flock of ibises straight out of the Bible
Take off and colonize

The wetlands beyond,
White bodies speckling
The brilliant green swamp
As I turn towards the penguins

Who will welcome me home,
Their sign growing larger as
I follow the road
Down into the shadows,

Tall trees lit up either side
By the Sun taking the whole
Sky, glowing like a bronze
medallion

Past the fence post with
The baby’s gumboot on it
And the balga mixing with
black burnt out trees,

The sealed ruts on Dixon Road
Shine too bright to look at
Like a non-stop river of mercury
Flowing out into the horizon.

Soon the smell of the sea
Will hit my nose and
The lights off Garden Island —

Qin Shi Huang could not
have imagined
this:

Immortality
In a single sunset.
