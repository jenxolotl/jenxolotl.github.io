Wireless Hill

At Wireless Hill there was once
A high and mighty radio mast,
That must have towered
Over the river below

I never saw it, but only its shadow
The iron silhouette of a civilization
Drawn in the wake of silent waves
The lightning lines

Finding their way through
Primordial earth
To make ground in a new land
To make the centre of everything

Into a corner of the world
The regular shapes and
Straight unsealed roads
And rising steel scaffolds

A pattern the same as anywhere
A place like nowhere else
And the signal has faded now
Into the noise of a greater whole

Into nothingness
Into its own shadow
Into the warmth of the sun
And the crackle of dried leaves
