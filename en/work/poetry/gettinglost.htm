Getting Lost

Out in the pine grove
My steps go farther than
They seem; turning round
In the shadow of a leaning giant

I expect to see the scars of settlers
But meet the rising wall of my imagination:
A network of young trees come together
To welcome me to nature, and induce in me the terror

That I go looking for, the thrill of the sojourner
With no intention to actually remain here,
Under the protection of the guardian spirits
I pray to the wirn wer nyittingur

To get me out of here,
And see the glint of my car,
An emaciated jarrah trunk illegally logged
And an old carton of Emu Bitter

Despite my pretensions
This land is pretty nice to me
This Wadjela noitj,
This misguided djanga.
