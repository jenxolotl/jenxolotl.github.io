Jarrah Forest, Summer Day

The repeating roar of chopper blades
Splitting the atmosphere rips through
The brightly shining green leaves
In the branches above, and the emergent saplings

Catching the warm rays of the Sun:
A thundering orchestra circles a distant point
Somewhere beyond the towering wall of trees
That were to guarantee my peace and quiet

A faintly visible taint of smoke
Suggesting the chance of real danger somewhere
Beyond the path of the choppers unclear enough
Through the canopy to leave it in doubt;

Out here with certainty comes the possibility of resignation

The deafening roar retreats back the way it came
As I walk farther along the leaf covered track
Into true silence, the sound of the ringing in my ears
I haven’t heard for years and even that too fades

Without the wind to hide it, the sweat flows
Behind my ear, and down the river of my back

In the man-blackened trunks a distant cry
Of a kookaburra is muffled into
The softness of a feathered pillow in the crunching steps
Of a kangaroo the creaking springs

Of a backyard trampoline —
I was always scared to go higher
As the little seedling with twelve leaves
In front of me aspires to a greatness

Beyond my comprehension
