Leaving the City (Homage to McComb)

I hope these sunglasses hide my eyes well enough
From the people whose stares could shatter
The glass, and slice both my balls in half
My heart is hypervigilant for jagged shards

And my stomach drags somewhere along the rails below
Looking for a squalid pit to lie in
With the entrails of my feelings dragging out
In a long stinking heap back towards the city,

Kissed by flies as they shrivel in the Sun,
Too haughty and arrogant to let the tears flow
In front of these strangers, these automatons
Who share no minds of their own

I am deliberately a small and pathetic sight, a smear
Of bird shit streaked down the Metronet logo
A man offers me a bottle of water and my traitorous lips
Betray the hint of a smile;

A sure sign that my determination to be petty and bitter
Is failing me, and even the blunt force fronts of trains
Don’t hold the allure they once did
A little boy pesters his dad about forbidden fruit

The day time burns weathered dots in my pasty skin
Same as always, as yesterday, as tomorrow.
