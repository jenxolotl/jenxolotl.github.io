Hypothermia

In the dark unyielding silence
In a gully by the road
Between two fields that stretch
To fill the pale blue earth

My fingers shiver across my body
The fold between two sheets
Encapsulating a lifetime, unable to sleep
Under the eternal passage of the stars

My feet made numb
By the cold that sweeps
Silently over the land,
Condensating the tears of ages

To a sleep that is not sleep
And dreams that are not dreams
But jumbled images that tumble
Helplessly from my eyes into

Opaque colourless pools
That lie in clusters all around
The flatlands of eternity and I too
Could join them, my skin

The warmth of glaciers

Before the morning comes and from
The rolling mist the familiar
Red dirt and gum leaves
Of my imagination
