spring sunday evening

Fading powdery sunlight
Your colour leaching out into
The edge of dusk, the precipice
Of my weekend

Coming near, the sounds of dinner
And overworked mothers playing
Past the gunshot patter of the hoons
Having their last joyride

And the wind chimes tinkling gently
As you fade, still,
Into faint shadows on palm leaves
And roaring engines on Read Street

The warmth vanishing from the air
And the birds crying in pity
With the kids back to school
The clock ticking away plenty —

If only you would
Stay with me a while longer
Dry my washing and
Together we could set a flame

Under this husk of a suburb
Driving diving falling slipping
To the horizon, we could
Burn this place down

And head over east, but
I don’t like the sound of pokies
And toll roads and pollies besides
I know you will return

Sooner and brighter,
To warm me again
