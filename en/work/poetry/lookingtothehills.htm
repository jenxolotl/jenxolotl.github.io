Looking to the Hills

The fog descends and covers the road
With the thin white shadows of past lives
Seen, and lost again in the mists,
The hours and minutes hardly visible

And only the pale red circles
Hold me back from careening into
The cross covered trees
In Kalamunda’s Valley of Death;

The sky etched in baroque cameo above
Trying to break the yellow monotony
Of a people trapped in their phones
And wandering aimlessly from job to job,

Their faces worn and cracked
Past the Canning River out to the badlands
Where those who never had a chance
At tertiary credentialled glory

Spin their wheels late into the night,
The aerial smoke rising to join
What is left of the stars above
This place, the cradle smotherer of dreams

In the arms of liveability
The road continues endless
But I prefer the country
Where I’m surrounded by cows and wheat

And not a spick amongst specks
But amongst nothing;
Where the emptiness
Makes me feel whole.
