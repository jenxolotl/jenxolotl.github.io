Confession

I laugh after funerals,
And take glee between the scenes
Of so many fragile humans,
Barely begun in being,

Rended to pieces that flutter
Between the ashes that burned
In my mind, but between breaths
I feel fine,

Smiling and laughing at jokes
In the space between friends;
A flash in the kitchen and
It's all gone, and I can’t comprehend

How I still smile like the others
All smiling, all laughing
In our little segmented corners
While the coffins stream past,

By twos, by fours,
What's after eight? Will I
Watch a movie, or take
A pleasant walk

While they all die around me,
As I dance, inches from their graves?
My life goes on as usual
And I grow immune to the casual

Forgetting of people’s names;
All the slaves of the same
Singing and laughing that trap me
Like all souls on this passing world —

It is a hopeful laugh because
I look behind at the cemetery
And it stretches still infinitely and I,
If I knew it,

Would do nothing but cry.
