Cape Le Grand

Looking out at Thistle Cove
Where the red rocks come to nearly meet
In their near-embrace of the brilliant blue sea
Glowing in its contrast,

The land draped in skimpy rags
Like a Grecian goddess seen from afar,
The waves pounded into smooth sheets of foam
Backed by a turquoise lather
That stretches out along the crystalline sand

The backpackers, travellers, and locals
Come and go in their ones, twos, and threes
Holding hands, sunglasses on their hair
Clothes billowing in the winds,
Skin turning gentle shades of red

They all look happy to be here
And I am happy too, in this place
Where everything prospers in the light

Climbing Frenchman’s Peak
Was once a daunting task
But it is all now so easy
I might do it twice;

My heart is etched on this coast
Free and uninhibited
