Kiss

Twice now I have seen the two weary lovers
Running past me on the train at the end of their shift

The RPH nurse with a smiling face name tag on her shirt,
And the man with luscious curls of hair tumbling over his fingers

Into palm, into tightly pressed skin only beginning to show its age

And still so beautiful both the two of you
Phone screen balanced precariously between your knees
Earphones on either side and foreheads lightly touching

The exertion in tired eyes intent on making a common space
I see you twice turn and kiss with defiance and resignation

Ashamed to look at your lips, the site of quiet resistance
The intimacy of daily life turned into a public spectacle by a machine
