After Dark

At night, the city the sound of ghosts
Lurking in the corners of forgotten rooms
Waiting, the deathly hum of street sweeping
Machines strange and beeping break

The silence that fills the still air
Echoing meandering through ominous
Alcoves, black corridors the cars
Wait intently for sleeping

Masters heavy eyes watching
Impaled on steel bars, spikes
Ready to pounce, lights
On the road counting down for

No-one, cross now, get out
Darkness all around, an LCD
Billboard of a girl transfixed
On something unseen

Too awful to speak then
Somewhere a scream, feet
Quickening, past the pale glow
Of Myers, occultic emanation

Cat whistle loud like a
Bullet through Forrest Chase,
Again, silhouettes of cacti
Getting closer, exposed

In the open
Gates barred down dead
Stairs pray for an exit
Panopticon grid, expecting

A visit from sinister forces
Don't look, lights searching
For blocks in advance
A squad of cops with guns

And armour swarming on
A man pleading his innocence
Not speaking, menacing,
Smashed wine bottle

Bleeding down the drain
More yelling, bottles breaking
A vacuum cleaner continues
Somewhere faintly unfazed

Lone traveller keeps trundling
Down the street threatening
To be swallowed by immense
Alleyways inviting, the barriers

Between worlds, caverns
Descending into concrete abyss
Get out now walking faster
Clutching his suitcase tightly

Homeless hiding flat in
Doorways like corpses big
Truck hurtles full speed
To escape this place

Getting cold now, the chill
Drawing under the skin
Swaying bodies down
The road seen for miles

Something is heard
Take a turn and descend
The small streets
Alone, far from people

Stay out of the night
Locks everywhere, sharp
Edges small lights failing
To contain something

Spilling out a ragged
Woman follows, drowned
In the sound of gangster
Rap thundering from

Her ghetto blaster, splintering
The fragile peace sound
Slamming against indifferent
Masonry, spectral contours

Of the river emerging,
A glimmer of a refuge
Parks leeched of colour
After the people left

The waters promising
Death to those who
Need it, the blue car,
A little warmth,

Engine on, the wheels turn
And cross the bridge
Toward the morning,
Another day, new light
