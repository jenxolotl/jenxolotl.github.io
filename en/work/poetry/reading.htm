Reading

I linger long and fast
On the faltering scraps of paper
That cross my eye,

The torn and ragged words
Of thoughts and feelings reduced
To their milieu;

The green and pleasant land
Of moles and piss-mires
Building turrets in the sand

Without regard for social advances
In the twentieth century
Such as McDonalds

Or the twenty-first
Where close-up shots
Of people’s arseholes

Are available on demand,
An apocalyptic literature
For the roadhouses

And skeevy magazine stands
That stick in my imagination
Almost as obscene as

The blasting of the Buddhas
The divinity of absence
In empty thrones

Reigning over the great
Modern world of meaningless
Polysemy —

The old words hold weight
Still precisely in the way
I found them;

Yellowed and paper-softened
By years of repetition, their
Dust filming my fingers

With the hope that
My work, too, will break
Apart and settle down

Into unfamiliar eyes,
Made strange by the loneliness
Of seeing a human experience

Rinsed and repeated
