Gaza, a fragment

When the rockets flew overhead in their thousands
Crossing the clear sky unimpeded
By the checkpoints, barriers, booklets, and bullets
That pin down our aspirations

For a moment I felt the joy of liberation
Of fences falling and people surging free
To be themselves, the great release
Of a century of suppressed feelings

The realization of our common dream

يا فلسطين بلادي
من البحر إلى النهر
يا فلسطين الحبيبة
سوف تكون حرة

A fantasy in high-explosive Technicolour
Coming to you live in never before seen revolution
A flurry of harmless firecrackers ringing in
The New Year for all the people cheering

It was the forceful kick of those who longed to breathe
And when I saw it connect to the face of
The occupiers, I felt that strength and blinked
And saw again that smoke would rise

To choke the toxic air;
Soaring lights would surely fall
Hurting when they hit the ground
My God, they’ll kill them all

While we sit like dazed idiots
Chained to the indifference of our screens
Softly sobered by the gravity of it all
International Relations, a game of white men

Always pressing down and forcing pins and lines
On our soft spots, of the human kind
I barely had time to mourn the coming dead
Ideals of kibbutzim, lost in time

On the straight path from Pearl Harbour to Okinawa
The pain multiplying in kind as no mercy
Becomes a virtue, a decency extinguished
With the wail of sirens and cries of loved ones

The crushing sound of bulldozers rolling in
And pencilled lines on old paper maps;
A tsunami of blood about to flow
From the sky and drown the whole

Of Palestine, my country, in the red sea of tears
The lingering hope once again giving way to fear
As the tide recedes, and we all brace
For the coming wave, the new storm of steel

Rising and leaving Gaza in its shadow, a husk
To be swept away to make a point about what is
Right, the mass murder of others, the bombs
Won’t leave a mark in broken homes, lives left in dark

But the city always had colour to me
Growing up, and seeing the parallel lines
Running between the story of their home and mine
Exploding to get out of the stifling silence

The quiet violence of unmentionable things
Surging through the vessels of life, our worlds
Meeting in the eyes of my friend, the future doctor

Her pride evident in the pretty picture and proper
Decor, her photogenic smile, still filling me with warmth
Though it's been a while since we talked, her words
I want to be a doctor so I can help Palestine

Still ring in my ears, and I wish she could’ve stayed
In my life, though I don’t know which is better,
The sudden violence of the blast or the long sadness
Whether she is at al-Shifa or some other hospital

Or by the gates of Rafah, still trapped
With only her strength of spirit to give her peace
In a world where family is a liability and
Talent targeted by the roaming fighter jets

That seek with the tap of a screen to block out
The stories of generations, still drowning out
The droning bombs that never cease, in the news,
Her fate the fate of Palestine, and I love her still

As when I met her and felt her blood pumping
Through the copper veins, the network of an unjust world
That keeps us together despite the effort to cut it all away
To make us silent in ourselves as the killing carries on

All around, my solitude is over and I see her
Every time I read the lines on people’s faces
Her words and smile still shaping my view
More than a decade on, the genocide rages

Unabated, and hers is just one of the multitude
Of treasures, the antique vases and intricate
Mosaics of living things that thoroughly underlie a place
Torn between empires for ten thousand years,

A land in the corner where the people suffer most
For the sins of distant leaders, and colonial misadventure
The belief that Arab blood is inferior
Makes the cities glow red like Roman glass

And Greek bird tiles, recently unearthed
I wonder if anything could remain
In a place where every street is combed over
With gunshots and the roads are filled with graves

As the children’s names compete for space
In the walls of text, denser than
The calligraphy lost in ruined mosques
And no less the tragedy than the adults

Their bodies piled high upon the spoiled flour
The desperate hunt for love and sustenance
As Israelis shoot them like ducks in a circus game
The Americans the ringleaders twirling their batons

As the world, dazzled by the spectacle, moves on
And swipes on, another clip, to the next gasp
Cruelly saying ‘never again’ before it even ends
The blood from Arab lands spilling over the fence

A drop on every hand, a drop gone from the rainbow
Sea of life in Gaza, Palestine, precious world
Their goal to make it something like Haifa
A city stripped of history and people

To be a tourist trap for outsiders
The Disney version of what is safe
For investors, populated by fugitives
In shattered homes and stolen houses

I just want to see the murder stop
So the surviving kids can play
Again, the adults rebuild
Dreams from ashes

To see their elders dip their toes
In the waters of forbidden shores.
