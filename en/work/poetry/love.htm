Love

Do I lie

When I say I love you
I think I love you
I believe I love you

Is it a snake oil
I have sold myself
To find a false safety

In the desire, fear, and vulnerability
A soft rug draped on jagged rocks
Of my own feelings

Shifting, unstable, multifaceted
Projections of my own psyche
Grasping reaching seeking

Semblance of an absolute
Reality
To take false comfort in

Perhaps you are a stranger
To me, and my proclaimed love
Is an insult

To your ineffable nature
My unknowing
Forever beyond my grasp

You, beyond my ideal
Behind the kisses,
The warm curves of your body

Beside my desire to know
To make you smile, to give
Without selfishness, selfishly

My words and intentions
A crude facsimile, a childish aspiration
Toward what you are

So effortlessly.
