Conventional Rhythm

There are those to whom I say
I miss you, I love you,
I think of you,
And the warm contours of your lips
Rising and falling to match

The rounded curves of your breasts
And the soft downy hairs caught
Against the light, against
Your fair white skin and the faint
Blue shadows of veins flowing like rivers

Into the pool of desire, my selfishness,
My want to go deeper into your abyss
And feel it surround me, drown me,
Destroy me in the sweet release
And leave me gasping for breath;

But are these words not a waste
Of air, what is left of my precious life;
The porn mag embedded in my book
Of poetry, against conventional images
Steaming mugs, the glint of toasters
