It’s Raining in Fremantle

It’s raining in Fremantle
Where I am, now
Silty water flowing down
Smoothed out masonry
Filling the ruts and tired contours

With a hundred years
Of pent up feeling;
A fetid stink
Latent, unbreathing
Slow seeping melancholy

It’s raining in Rockingham
Where you are, now
Beads of light lit up
By the white hand of God
Burst through the clouds:

Pooled by the roadside,
You, my imitation David
In grey Italian marble
Your figure cut in two,
Fingers split

Against the swing
Of rubber wipers
Cleaving a path
Through this moist air;
Pressed tight against
Your form,

Tired eyes through
Tempered glass.
