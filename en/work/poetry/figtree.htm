The Fig Tree

You took me and told me
Look, Jenny, it's a fig tree
You can tell by
the broad green leaves

That this is something
useful, precious
In our own back garden
That I have wandered through
For years without realizing

You climbed the heights
And went higher
And took me, open eyed
To see the stars unscreened

From the tree where you look
Like a little monkey, or
a bird in its nook
Foregrounding life around you

Try it, Jenny
You said to me
Peeling back the green rind
To reveal sweet white tendrils
And red flesh inside

Slivers of deliciousness like
Gummy worms
But the packet is not plastic
And having a picnic with you

Under the fig tree
In our own park
As we watched the passers by
Look not at our plentiful bounty

I felt that being human
Could be sweet.
