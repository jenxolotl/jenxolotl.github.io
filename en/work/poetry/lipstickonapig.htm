Lipstick on a Pig

The deadliest thing is plastering
Blackfulla words on whitefulla ways:
Freshly erect signs and strange mouth shapes
To pay a paltry lip service
To a story old beyond words

Like a feeble blowjob
Given in a back alley
Behind some broken needle nightclub
Unable to arouse the passions
Of a violently awakened mob;

Mixing without mixing
The stale colonial lobster shell
Made more perverse by
The fringe of primordial blakness
It wears like a garland of skulls;

Beautiful in its juxtaposition,
Like lipstick on a pig:

The pig does not tarnish the lipstick;
The lipstick does not pretty the pig.
