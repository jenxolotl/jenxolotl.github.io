Come with me to Northbridge and we’ll sing and play the drums all night

Come with me to Northbridge,
And we’ll sing and play the drums all night
We’ll waltz under the William Street rainbow,
And dance on the grass in Russell Square
We’ll eat sago at the Old Shanghai and try our hand at chicken feet
We’ll head down Francis Street and clink balls and glasses, shooting pool to the tune of Taiwanese TV

Come with me to Northbridge,
And we’ll sing and play the drums all night
We’ll trade war stories under flame heaters
And talk to queens and dykes under the smoky haze at Connies
We’ll go to strip shows and eat kebabs and meat boxes at holes at one in the morning

Come with me to Northbridge,
And we’ll sing and play the drums all night
We’ll hold hands looking at the city glow
And weave through the throng of beggars, bouncers and revellers
We’ll walk across the empty plaza toward the lonely skybridge and traino

Come with me to Northbridge,
And we’ll sing and play the drums all night
We’ll kiss and curse the coming week, the rising sun,
The passage of time, police sirens out of sight
We’ll part our ways and have a shower, go to bed, back to work
We’ll take our stock, we’ll stay in, we’ll grow old, the inevitability of the light.
