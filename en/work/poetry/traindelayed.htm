Train Delayed

I am a slave to a clock, a wristwatch
My shackle to a schedule
Beyond my own making,
Without meaning forcing me to gather

With the rest of the downtrodden
Masses thinking derogatory comments
Probably held mutually, at the
Same time same place same thing

Same damn dog pissing on the garden
Bed of my life over and over,
The pretty flowers on faux enamel faces
Covering the unpleasant rash where

Skin meets raw metal, another incident
Echoed over cold concrete into
The incomprehensibility of grown men
In green polyester elf suits

Stumbling around the platform
In drunken confusion, eyes looking
Blankly around to put lie to the
Orange numbers on the board

Train delayed again, Monday
Morning getting closer like
A hard surface under the influence
Of gravity feeling faster

And farther from the safety
Of space than before,
Time crunching to a singularity
In the exhaustion and frustration

And body giving up
To the moment made forever,
The stars above the city lights
The quiet calm in suburban streets
