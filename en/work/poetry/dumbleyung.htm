Dumbleyung

Standing alone on the summit
Overlooking Lake Dumbleyung
Stretching endlessly into the distance
Like the open sky
And the yellow stalks of cut wheat

Here the only sounds
Are the whispering rustle
Through their stems and
The crunch of the salt
Under my feet

I look at Dumbleyung and see Campbell
Racing across the lake, his
Path marked on a little toy boat
I read his final words (at Coniston), and say

He surely knew he would die
Though he probably didn’t
Have much time to think about it

My solitude is broken
By the bite of a march fly
And the arrival of a throng of British tourists;

I am twenty-nine today.
