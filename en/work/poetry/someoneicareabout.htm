Someone I care about is leaving town soon

Someone I care about
Is leaving town, soon
At the airport,

Where the tarmac runs down
Past the brown grassy fields
Where the immense metal tubes

Defy gravity for a moment,
Shaking the air with
Their butter churning engines,
Ready to pull the willing

To new worlds, far beyond
The blue seas and red dirt
And powder moon rising quietly
Behind the turbojet wail

And clicks of shutters
And Carnaby cries
Above the tree-topped hills
Of Kalamunda, face caressed

By the long screen of smoke
That seems to hang there forever
At this time of year
Wafting far beyond the bushlands,

And hilly bike trails
The manicured ovals
With empty soccer nets
And concrete basketball courts

With rusty hoops
Waiting for the bounce of the ball
And the shudder of the backboard
Reverberating through

The winding roads of the suburbs
Along the trickling rivers and babbling
Streams with grassy banks lined
By old peeling paperbarks

And the quacks of wide eyed ducks
Over the white railed footbridges
That broach wreaths across
The dead straight thoroughfares

And the continuing flow of traffic,
Red, silver, and black,
Passing east to west to Fremantle,
Out to the ships

Laying their weight at anchor
And the tourists waltzing
Up and down the coffee strips
To the clink of mugs

And the polite chatter
In a hundred tongues
The roar of the engines
Claims the warm air

Gently blowing and spirals
Down the runway
To a lingering whine
That remains on the horizon

At the heart of it all, the hordes
Of people in business suits
And one piece dresses,
Distressed jeans with dangling chains

And dyed hair blowing
Under the steady crosswinds
Of the towering grid
Of glass towers, the ground’s

Compass to the stars

And the vast expanse beyond
The emptiness around this town
The farms and plains and sprawling waves
That someone that I care about

Is rushing past,
Into the shimmering beauty
Of the twilight sky
Backed by the golden glow

Of the Sun in the West,
Catching a glint
On rising sheet metal
And returning it all

To my open eyes.

An ant singing the glory of its anthill,
A bird patiently arranging
The twigs in its nest,
The green bus stops and CBD skyline

Seem tall and omnipresent here
But in the air quickly shrink
To insignificance; the long freeways
The pretty Christmas lights

To celebrate new adventures
In strange places,
Beyond the hills,
In the world beyond.
