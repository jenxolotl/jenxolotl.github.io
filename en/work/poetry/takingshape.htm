Taking Shape

Look at you begin to grow
Your graceful curves, soft and delicate
For this refined age, rising
High above the grit and soot

Of working toil, the thousands
Of men all devoted to you
Clanking, and clattering,
Clamouring with grease and sweat

To be part of your story
O you beautiful ship emergent
Rising triumphant in your vast
Incubator, an Empire yours to sail

From the finest Scottish steel,
The apple of British earth
Ingrained within you,
The pride of Belfast and the Gaels

Your grace belies your strength and power,
Magnificent beast of the oceans
To be, reinforced keel
To dwarf the whales,

Women, and colonial wealth,
Left behind in your wake
As your skilfully mustered shape
Cuts through the largest difficulties

Like a knife through tea cake
Yet to be cut, the growing
Empty spaces within you full
To the brim with a civilization

At its pinnacle, the greatest age
And latest in modern safety design
Infusing you with noble mind,
The bodies of workers

Like crawling ants clamber
About your frame, your hull
An imposing steel wall insurmountable
By human aspiration, yet so gentle

In a photo of a modern marvel
Bringing the West together
In unprecedented luxury, the dawn
Of a transcendent day.
