I hesitate to give my name over the phone sometimes

I hesitate to give my name
Over the phone, sometimes,
For the reaction I know I’ll get
Understated, subliminal
The force of a lifetime found
In a single syllable

“Oh,”

The hesitation, and continuation
Of conversation
Awkwardly hindered by
The piercing of the veil,
My naked flesh to strangers
Exposed, the embarassment

Of an adam’s apple
Poking around corners
To show a bit too much
Of my physical form

But the unspoken recognition
Lingering, hesitantly,
Breaking the smooth flow
Of bonds I never agreed to

Feels better than hearing
The wrong inflection, and turn of phrase
Reflected back to me, my own fear
Of my voice failing, dropping
Into the wrong gear and
Getting painfully comfortable

Awkward as it feels
They sometimes address me
Tentatively, hesitantly, correctly.
