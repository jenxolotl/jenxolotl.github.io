Afternoon Piece

Into the fading light of the day
The coffee filter satin sheets laid out
And hung to dry, the crinkled
Ranges on coloured maps in National Geographic books

And lines forming in the off-white
Streetlamps glowing below Lesmurdie
Plane rising from the airport
The rivets along its wings faintly ruled

Like a mediaeval manuscript translucent
Vellum flapping two stroke engine
The water flowing down the muddy village path
And into the brilliant Vịnh Hạ Long

Shining rocks mother eagle keeps
The children away tears frozen in the
Land lying kangaroos sleeping
Beneath the retreating cloud

And it is gone again
Golden glow the dusk delayed
In the rising tide
Crashing against the rocks

Under a full moon
At Point Peron
