Leap Day

It’s an odd kind of day
when it comes around every so often
like a distant aunt, not the cool one
who lets you address them by first name

but not the worst and when
they come around and show you
their standard shots of Damascus
and Cairo

Cut off on colour slides, standing
awkwardly around the old
monuments to tradition
A life lived on schedule

where variation is a sin
unless it is ordained in
some limited circumstance;
as it is when the weight

of February limps and keels
over a bit into the heft
of March, that mighty month
where tsars are murdered and

Spring finally begins;
that is, unless you’re in
the south where a long spell
of darkness lies ahead

even if there will be chocolate
easter bunnies, mass produced
and tin foiled, a mass movement
for a mass market that

allows no inconsistencies but
those ordained by God; and
occasionally a day that comes
and goes away because our

most mechanical systems
cannot handle the infinity
of being
