Solitude (Petrudor #1)

Why am I out here

At the fringe of the world
Where the ground is scorched
Dry and hard, and even
The flowers seem sharp and alien

What am I attempting to prove
In this unrelenting silence
The rays of the sun
And swarming flies

My only persistent company
In this barren land,
Beautiful though
It may be,

My car an alter persona
In the absence of family,
Friends, the feeling
Of home lasting disconnect

Without a signal I can hide
On the frontiers of adventure
Solitude a pretence for loneliness
But if I can confront my feelings

Out here, I could do it
In Rockingham and perhaps then
The three-hundred kilometre journey
Was pointless

But at least I get the stories,
Pretty pictures, and a night
Spent under the stars.
