Snowflakes on a Quiet Evening

Snowflakes float slowly down,
Falling from their castles in the sky.
Thin solar rays illuminate their design;
Radiant as the front piece of a crown.

I watch and breathe an enamoured sigh
At the sight on mortal soil of something infinitely divine.

Their colour being the purest white,
Blinding me with intensity from Sun unseen;
Filtered light showing the frailty inside
These little prisms dropped from great height.

How arduous their journey must have been
At the mercy of nature: the wind to the trees and the moon to the tide.

The last of the snowflakes end their graceful dance;
The sky turns crimson, the air grows chill.
The sun prepares for celestial slumber.
I reluctantly arise from my seated stance

— The spectacle over, the neighbourhood still —
And with great alarm see the car is snowed under.
