Mt Cooke (Good Friday)

It’s been such a long time since
I rose up the iron-rich arteries
Running through your arms,
Into the midst of your bosom,

A gorilla

Heaving and panting, never failing
To get lost all over again in erst familiar
Passageways made strange again by
New angles, new colours, new lines

You never cease to amaze me with your ability
To surprise and ambush my overconfidence
With rough edges that demand respect —

Over and over, you draw blood from my tender legs,
Imbue me with the fear that brings life,
At the summit you summon punishment
For my hesitation, and

Daring me to take what is mine
Reward me for my commitment, my love
For you, embedded in the sweet eucalypt
Leaves and views without parallel

The ripened palm seeds
Fall in tangled clumps
To the hard and waiting ground;
After I have forgotten your ways,

You give the feeling back to me anew
