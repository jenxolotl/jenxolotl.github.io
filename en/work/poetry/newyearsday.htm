New Years' Day

I saw the way your little paws poised
Tentatively, in white socks,
Like a kid awaiting Santa
And we watched

The car loom menacingly close
On the eve of new year
The way I pensively approached
Made you swallow the doubt

Written all over your face
And boldly cross my path
You knew your mistake
My foot hit the pedal

And you slowed your step
To the harsh screech of brakes
Against the hard road
As my brand new tyre, and

Your terrified eyes, transfixed
Made forlorn plans to meet at a point
And merge dimensions
As one year crossed into the next.

I didn't want to look
Before I had time to feel
The flesh underneath
And thud after squeal

But there was silence —

No thump,
And emptiness in mirror
The only sound
The growing beat of my heart

I know, as you know
You had cheated death
Although I turned to be sure
The path behind was blood-free

So the future was clear

I watched the fireworks
Shredded to bright little pieces
Seeing your eyes as they met mine,
O black cat,

And the seconds ticked over
And time stopped.

I think you certainly lost
One of your lives
As I purged the year's karma
Viscerally
