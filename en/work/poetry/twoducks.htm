Two Ducks (2024)

A pair of ducks waddle up the bank to see me,
Going slowly on their stumpy legs,
Hiding their curiosity as poorly
As I do my own

They peck the ground, idly
Looking without looking
One twists its neck and nestles
Its bill into its back feathers,

Looking straight at me,
Streaks of iridescent green
Running down its side,
And as I think about the way

The black lines about its eyes,
Running gracefully down its face,
Remind me of a maiko looking bashful
In the old part of Kyoto

The other stretches its neck out
And bites its partner on the arse
They waddle back to the water with a start
And I realize they’re

Not so different after all.
