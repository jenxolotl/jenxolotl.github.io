i chose to be an orphan

i chose to be an orphan
rather than join them, the oppressors
with jokes that slice like knives
through the throats of the dispossessed

the voiceless, they called them
but they sound so loud and clear
from where i’m standing, the margins
of mercenary society invisible to those

who would choose not to listen
or cannot because the levers are
pressed firmly in their cold dead hands
and i cannot pry them off and i

don’t know how i got away
from dirty grabbing fingers
all i can say is that i chose
to run away from the screens

and factory stalls promised
freely to me to try to meet
other people on the level
where i could, the world my family
