Easter

The weight of imported tradition from
An inverted world that drags me down
With its pornographic imagery, of
Gay colour and the bunnies
Bouncing happily around

Cute though they may be,
An unwanted species in the growing darkness
And stupid cartoon grins
In shop windows, pretending
The death of us all

The Earth will end with a special offer
Set for the wrong season,
The wrong place,
Your existence an anomaly here
Unless you’re a shattered human

Being living in the gaps
Between glass walls
And the crypts we build ourselves
On God-forsaken clearings

Life a shadow, a backwards reflection,
This broken feeling always coming
Back to me right when I feel

Like I’m doing okay,
the blue burning out
To black never far away, it follows
You everywhere here.

Light giving way can’t escape
Even the roads lead to nothing
Worth seeing, the nocturne stillness broken
By wailing sirens and seppo hydras
and spouses screaming

Late in the night, the stars fading behind
The cankered city light, this feeling
Still haunts me when I’m alone, and
Hurts the way it did

When I was young and too weak
To open my eyes, a blind bird sheathed,
Tired of being mired in the spectre
Of a rancorous crowd

Of cockies arguing over where to roost
The faintest glimmer of pink
Still visible if only in my mind
The crickets, too, chirping

Their desires thoughtlessly,
Looking to fill the night
With something else, a little brown figure
Scurries past, on my old stone pavers,
I recognize the hoons

Running their rounds with firecracker energy
Neither thinking too much, nor doing too little
Just getting where they need to be
And I understand now

The darkness, a fleeting scene
Unfolding cosmic tragedy before me
And I still feel the beats of the play
As you are meant to

When you watch a good movie,
Wrapped up in blankets for your comfort,
The lover is optional, I’m somehow excited, the lights dim
For the coming Easter

The passion lived out again
