Blood Splattered Passport

A blood splattered passport
In the pages of tonight’s news
The views raising the stakes
Up to eye level; a war

Best forgotten boomeranging
Back into public imagination
Dangled from gloved hands
Sterile, the evidence still dripping

A kangaroo and emu
On a field of blue
With a background of
Genocide grey

I used to have one just like it

Though my name was changed
And the colour doesn’t suit me now
How it stands out from the others
Western too, a special kind of blood

That drives the PM into a frenzy
And seems to drive our national policy
But congeals the same as all
The others, on bloodied paper

I’ve seen the Palestinian ones
And the colour’s a bit darker
And font a little thicker
But functionally the same

When do they get their phone calls?
You would need a truckload
Or a diverted plane
To carry all the blood

Being spilt in our name.

Dedicated to the memory of Lalzawmi ‘Zomi’ Frankcom — Killed by an Israeli air strike in Gaza on April 1st, 2024.
