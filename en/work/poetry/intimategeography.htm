Intimate Geography

How could I not love you,
Your gently sloping curves
Winding through the landscape,
Undulating, heaving with the steady rhythm
Of your breath, brushing over
The downy hairs on your lip,

Red and full of blood,
Flowing into me with every touch
Of your soft skin, the warmth
My mattress every time
I lay between your hips

At the edge of the grass,
Head nestled in the valley
Under your mounds, rising gently
From the plains of your ribs,

Giving slightly to the gravity
As I lay to rest upon you;
Whose arms embrace me
And encompass the whole earth

You have always been there for me,
The sound of brooks babbling through you
And the thump thump of your heart
In my ear, and echoed
Through my own.
