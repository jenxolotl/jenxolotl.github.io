Late Night Bus

In the quiet drone
When the night is done
The warm yellow glow
Of the street lamps

And the driver, motionless
In the darkened mirror
Drape me in tiredness
With a day that has nothing

Left to give but
The negative of an image
White on black
Of shuttered shops

And passing artificial glow
Of the signs like beacons
Past homes and the places
I used to know

The drone of the engine
Whirrs through the silence
Of this motley band of desperados
The last of a dying breed
Faces wearied and buried

In their phones, portals
To the familiar lines of home
And the colour in the blackness
That surrounds us all

In this humming metal box
Always rolling forwards
As my eyes drift away from
The songs of the parties

Towards the rising light
Of dawn, the final station
And home just over
The horizon.
