The Boys Racing Down in Wattleup

The pastoral scenes down Wattleup way
Are too much for my mind
To describe, with exhausted eyes and tired tongue
On my way back to start it all again

Like the loser of some unending game of Snakes & Ladders
Looking down at the board,
The bridled observer on Stock Road

The country streets look so small
And I can see the country boys
Come racing for miles
From my vantage point,

Past the farms and old buses
And rusting sheds on artificial hills,
Their racing speed looking like a gentle drift

Through the rural idylls
Of a scene JMW Turner
Would have loved to take
And I would have loved
To be in their boots

Pressing hard down and turning
Along another dirt road
I can see right down
The path of their excitement
And I am invisible to them;

As my road curves away
I hope there will always be
Another bend.
