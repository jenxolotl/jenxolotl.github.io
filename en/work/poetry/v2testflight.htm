V2 Test Flight

Up, up, and away
From the flimsy tower
And concrete concerns
Of the people who built it

The checker-board rocket
Leaves the earth in a blaze
Of glory, its geometric lines
And ballistic trajectory

A marvel of modern science
A confirmation of the numbers
And figures found in old books
Left by the launch-pad

Cold and indifferent
As it spirals away, above
A fire seen for miles and
The craters getting closer

As it flies away
To a vanishing point of light
And in minutes exceeds
All that has ever been known;

Alone in the cold black void
The curvature of
The world below and its origin
Lost in the confusion,

The war obscured
In the silence
Beyond observation,
Basking in the respite

For an eternal moment, weightless
Freed from the heft
Of our minds and tumbling
Back down

To the world that built it
Burning up in our air
Broken to bits
Against the unyielding sea

The moment lost
To little books and photos
The bones in starving camps
Faded slanted memories

To calculated strike targets.
