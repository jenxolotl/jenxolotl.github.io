Eternal Play

Long streaks of clouds
Criss cross the dawn sky,
Gently flowing past
The go-getters and rank opportunists

Now racing in the opposite direction
Lit light and dark
Like shadow puppets in the Ramayana
Play of the West,

A surreal tale of commerce
That repeats itself
Every morning in the popcorn galleys
Beneath the sight before my eyes

In every town, on every shore,
And every place continuing endlessly
As the Earth twists gently
Around the Sun,

Pure white in its brilliance,
Like splattered molten metal,
Its glory too hot to capture
In my camera lens

Or out the metal nib
Of my fountain pen.

A small consolation to glimpse
What lies before, and beyond
The eternal play outside my window
On the way to work again.
