inspiration jam

i didn’t get taught it at school
the old classics, zhuāngzǐ and sappho
an epicurean bent through my own
effort, juche tango sans hagiography

pulp fiction pre-tarantino those times
turned inside out, old paperback
editions traded on the playground
like skunk weed, sherlock holmes

jules verne, whatever strange new
word i could lay my eyes on
while all the other kids played
and the adults too at times

would put on something nice
drinking bottle after glass
to drown their sorrow in aqua,
kylie, prince, at last

i could steal the opportunity
to walk my own winding path
in the wake of old men,
far away, most of them dead

i knew too few women then

didn’t see what i needed to
until much later the light
of the buddha, flowing through
o wilde, d parker, st.

vincent millay getting closer
now but i’m still dead tired
at uni of taut textbook clichés
and talk of european holidays

big degrees and grants and
high pressure gigs while i suffer
just to take the train there,
perth proletariat my 老师

now i’ve read too much and still
importantly talked to the people
life ringing through them like
bakers, wrestlers, cleaners,

builders, painters, a weird
mob pulled together only
by their disunity, their will
to feel all things fully

while individual, the stranger
among crude cursed men
and white ivory tower types
now more connected than

where i was before
