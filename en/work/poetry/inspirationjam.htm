inspiration jam

i didn’t get taught it in school
the classics, zhuāngzǐ and sappho
an epicurean bent through my own
effort, juche tango sans hagiography

pulp fiction pre-tarantino time
turned inside out, old paperback
editions traded on the playground
like skunk weed, sherlock holmes

jules verne, whatever
i could get my hands on
while all the other kids played
and the adults too at times

would put on something nice
drinking bottle after bottle
to drown their sorrow,
inxs, prince, then i

took the opportunity
to walk my own path
in the shadows of old men,
far away, some of them dead

too few women then

didn’t see what i needed to
until much later the light
of the buddha, flowing through
o wilde, d parker, st.

vincent millay getting closer
now but i'm still dead at uni
tired of taut textbook clichés
and talk of european holidays

degrees and grants and
fancy jobs while i struggle
to catch the bus again
working class always my 老师

now i’ve read much and much more
importantly talked to the people
names yet unsung, writers,
wrestlers, cleaners, bakers,

acrylic painters, a queer
assortment brought to be
together only by some
shared strong will to feel things

fully, still individual, a stranger
among crude cursed men
and white ivory tower types
more connected than

i was ever before
