Riddle

The flies covet it
Trying to crawl into the corners of my lips
To seize it, as it flows through me
And spills ecstatically from the gash in my leg

The Sun burns its mark on it
The seal of the senses on my skin
Driving me forward with the whip of rebirth
To the next point of shade, the next line of my poem

Hands in the water of the billabong
Feet beating the long sandy track,
The stars glowing in my blisters,
The dusk setting seesaw my pangs for more

The Moon lusts for it, what I have in abundance
My words and ego limit it, boundless behind the trees
The sea gives rise to it, salt burning my open eyes
Everywhere its children, the flowers, the music,

The motorbike roaring past the corpse lying by the roadside,
The contorted shapes, the blue bags in concrete gateways
To beautiful bones, bleached white against the ground
To the smell of smoke and smouldering piles of ash,

My steady breath carries it; I cannot evade it
