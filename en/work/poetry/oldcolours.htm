Old Colours

In the cold autumn air as my breath rises in wisping clouds
I still evoke the warm memories of the things of you
Where my feelings once grew as lichens on a mountain branch
Appear still in changing winds

Embodied in the girlish shrill of excitement the powerful toasty gaze of a theatre light fixed confident beams in the core of my being

The smell of fresh gingerbread between the aisles of spudshed green banksias and smokebushes growing in bunches across the land for the feel of Freo without sleep at 3am

The rocky soil on which my heart took root and even more intangibly the feeling of pulsing radiating goodness emanating from distantly associated sources

Further, farther, father away from me now, after
The thunder cracks the sky and torrential rain washes it down the street
The remains of my images

In the lingering violet of the dawn air
The moon suspended softly in a train window
The fading of the colours into ordinary light

Through unstopping dragging speeding hours
Toward the rising scarlet of a dusk horizon
Where the repetition of a cycle can almost be seen

In the gentle curves of your surface
