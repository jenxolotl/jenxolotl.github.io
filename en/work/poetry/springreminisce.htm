spring reminisce

The sights of spring have come again
To the fringes of this degraded land,
The blood red ball of the sun
Casting a pall of flame in the sand

The roadside grasses sprouting seed
And taking to the wind,
Reminding me of younger days
Spent lounging under smoky gauze

And smell of burning eucalypts
The feel of blades in my skin
In the shadow of the gnarled tree,
At the end of the school oval

Where my nose ran heavily but I didn’t care
Because the flowers bloomed across the ground
And the gentle burn felt nice on my arms,
Lying flat on my back all afternoon

Languishing in the still hot air
And the sounds of dark birds,
When I could imagine the day
Would remain like this forever
