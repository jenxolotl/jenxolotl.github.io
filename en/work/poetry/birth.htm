Birth

I entered this world not far from here
In a little hospital on the edge
By the big brackish lakes,
Vast shimmering planes

A little past three,
Assisted in the exit by
A Sri Lankan medic whom
My mother never quite forgave

First breath by the dawn
Of sterile hospital air
I had scarcely seen light
When I was back on a flight

To the rich red earth
And dust draped domiciles
Of Newman, a bastion
In the State’s far North.

Stranger among strangers in a strange land
Where men worshipped a big hole in the sand
Scar-streaked and ravished
The hole in my heart

Surrounded by endless, distant expanse.

This was my home
And I looked up at the trucks,
Blocking the Sun,
With childish awe

The monstrous machines
And trains without end
Take on dimensions
Stretched beyond my horizons.

This would be my hole to descend
But my limit was the hill
Of ants down the road

I loved the dust, and dirt,
And dry spinifex brush

The cyclonic rains
Made mud, and dry cracked plates
Willy willies, all
Spinning in the ravine of civilization

I saw them clearly
In the cracks
Outside the barbecued verandahs
And sprinkled lawns

That defined our society,
Defying the norms and living betwixt
Old burnt out wrecks and
Broken bottles and barrels

On the fringe
Between the new rubber-footed order
And the greens, and reds
And crumbled bone-whites

Of unimaginably ancient land,
Calling me out to the great
Rainbow world beyond.

I saw them out there,
The Other Ones,
Like me but black in skin
And poor, with bottle in hand

I looked to my elders for support
Not knowing the reasons
Behind glowering stares
Harsh words said in malice;

Abdication of responsibility and
Moral cowardice

They told me to keep away,
That the world was my ant hill
But the seed was planted
And my trust was broken.

I was too young, then
And did not cross the curtain
Before returning with parents
To blue-watered Rockingham

Mooriburdup, always
The birthplace I’d forgotten

A white spirit of the dead lost
In the land between two countries
Between two salt lakes, silent
And the incredible sea beyond.

An alien now
In the land I was born

I felt alone from everyone
Sitting and crying on the floor of a bus
Berated by old men, and the gawking stares
Of wide-eyed schoolchildren.

Precocious before his age
An adult in a boy’s body
Terrible things to say to a child
Who doesn't know whether she’s coming

Or going to an inferno

In a world of fences, and barbed wire
Car tyres screeching through the night
Standardized tests and clocks slowed to a crawl
The clink of piled beer bottles

Drowning the calls of crows and cowed wives
Lost in a suburban cacophony

Dying in White, working-class
Hegemony.

I could not be one of them.
But what else could I be?

Nothing.
No one.

A congealed speck of grease, sitting
In an unwashed pan.

The darkness, blackness, swept over me
Like the pounding, unyielding waves at sea
What is a home if not a prison
What is a promise if not a lie

Nowhere and nothing

I gasped for air, struggling to catch
My breath, eyes darkened with despair
Trapped behind the doors, cheap meals
Panic attacks at sports fairs

Short breath found again

In the kindness of too few friends
Family visits, in smoky homes
The old books, and pictures
Of unfamiliar flags from places unknown

Sleepwalking through the saltlicked land
My eyes blind to the burning Sun
The blood of Lancelot and Guinevere
Running through me, far away

The dark and endless depths
Of a world wide web, undaunted,
Beckoned to me to come
Like the red rock and spinifex

Stretching to the horizon
Ensnaring me in its net
Like a seductive red back
Of infinite meaning

Under the stars; in the glow
Of the computer’s early light.

My world was a room.
My world was a sea of bits that
Could be arranged into
Any pattern of my choosing

If only I knew to hold that power
Without abusing the fragile folds
Of my mind, happy memories of Sun
And soil overwritten, physicality denied

Without the blistering heat outside,
The walls and fences of my world
Came tumbling down into
Cognitive void.

Everything and nothing
Everywhere and nowhere
Soon the names of every King of England
And some Queens came quick to my lips but

They remained parched of the taste of life.

How they became cracked and stained
By the dirt all around, shit on the ground
Still dogging me, disturbed, too sick to breathe
In a changing world, the light of childhood

Fast fading out

And growing the embarrassment, the first black hairs
That I hid feverishly, the signs of my shame
Full difference from the others, all changing
Budding breasts and broken voices, getting tall together

Like the barbed wire barriers, holding us all
Back in, the threat of violence, the blood spilling
On concrete and rushing to my ears, the boys shouted
Faggot, as I swam beneath the water

To avoid the girls’ gaze.

Lungs burning, eyes aching from looking
For the gleam of knives in pant pockets, the grey
Of locks on school gates, the cute smiles chained away
Of boys, and the girls too scary to consider

A reality of constant terror
Between the darkness;
First light.

The coming of the dawn again
Life found in the formation of bits
Mutuality across an intangible divide

Japan, a much grander place than Rockingham
To sense deprived eyes an alternative
To nine holes in the ground,
Six feet down, sixteen years past

A former golf course could not compare
To the grandeur of castles
And glimmering Shibuya
In the City of Light

I made friends anew, new furrows in the Earth
And soon replaced suburban mundanity
With nascent transnational sublimity,
At least in the corners of my mind,

A useless thing in a body bounded
By four walls and a little bird fountain
In a garden of regret.

I had to break out, before I rolled over
I had to break out, but a leap into fantasy

Is a leap against gravity
Back into the deep pools of life
Darkness, re-emergent

Despite the depths of Lake Biwa, the rolling
Stock sounds of Osaka, running past the clear
Shimanto river, and still looking further,
Afar, for the signs of something better

From Sakhalin, to Kalmykia, to the far
Familiar deserts of our own, the Navajo Plateau,
I looked and kept looking, finding without
Finding, across the other shore, the calm

In new China, and there I stayed, for years more,
Growing without growing, my life a glass house
Taking greedily the rays, and waiting for the stone
To shatter it all

Ah, ah

Break me into pieces and cure me
With your body, beyond description
I lived high through you for years
In the clouds of your mind, hurting

In your own way

Together in pain for a while, basking in
The calm before a storm that never came
The dream of electricity dying
As I grew older and opened my eyes,

Emerging slowly into the light, a newborn child
Been around the world too many times to count
A tired young body, inquisitive blue eyes
Out by the brackish lakes, and ready

To understand the image in the mirror:
A natural delivery.
