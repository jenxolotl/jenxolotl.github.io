Wellington Square

Nothing, and no one lies
Between the relentless rain
And the burning sun
Searing my skin

The images, the sounds
Sensations of a rhythmic life
Are like a hammock swinging
In a gentle breeze

As the ropes strain and squeak
Under the tiresome weight
They will surely break
But not today.

Not now, not here
Under the brilliant blue sky
And the soothing warmth of
Mother Sun.
