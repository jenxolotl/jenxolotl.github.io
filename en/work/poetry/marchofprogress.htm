The March of Progress

Time
Leeching away
It leaves me covered in marks and sores

As it draws from me
From every pore
And frozen fingertips
A mind fixed on the clock

No time
No time
Love, family, friends
Seem so far away
On the long march forward

Never backwards
Or you will drown like the others
The incomprehensible heaps of clothing
In the doorways and the gutters
Along the winding road

Everything must wait its turn
Always a next time
What about this time?

Does happiness lie
In the rising glow of the morning sun
The misty hills across the river
Or the smiling face

Of a passing stranger.
