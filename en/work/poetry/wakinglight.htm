Waking Light

The light filtered
Into finely-banded lines
Through the slats covering

The outside world,
The emptiness of my stomach
Tightens intensely, persistently

Against the intermittent chirping
Of the birds outside
A wattlebird clucks

To break the silence, sleep
In the corners of my eyes
It is morning again

And the adventure lies
On the other side
Of the glass
