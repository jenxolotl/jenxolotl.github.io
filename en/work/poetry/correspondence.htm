Correspondence

Every day, the same
Little honeyeater comes to visit
My home, in the garden
And I know he

Should I assume a bird’s gender?
I’m no ornithologist but
It was always personality that mattered
To me, was always the same

Because he stood at the same
Spot on my fence, as the Sun
Stood about thirty degrees
Above the horizon, bathing

His yellow cheeks in soft glow
The same as I saw him
Wear the mask, the man
On the Underground

Who followed me home
Covered in dust like
A refugee from Ground Zero
Standing in the same place

Each time the detritus falls
Like ash he cocks his head
And inspects my weed, coming
Home with my begotten seed

From the grey plight of East Perth
The image still burned in
My mind, the same platform
To the same flight,

To a world I was denied
The freedom of the bird
And the tired masked man
The terma of within

And without revealing
The non-duality of light
And dark, passing moments
Fixed in the motions

of the Sun.
