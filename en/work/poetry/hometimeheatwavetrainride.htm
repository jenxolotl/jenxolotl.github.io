Hometime Heatwave Train Ride

Heat hanging like a thick blanket
Used to smother a crying child,
Folded over itself, twofold, threefold,
In the dimly swirling fire-breeze

Of an air con feebly attempting
To maintain control, the commuters trample
On and on, prime white-collar beef prepped
For export and packed together
As tightly as humanly
possible

Inhumanly, a sense of desperation in the air
As awkward smiles come into contact with
Braised elbows and the yells of derros

Echoing flaccidly down the platform
More and more squeeze on, the aisle a tangled
Mess of limbs, the humidity rising, people trying
To look through shaded eyes anywhere but

The neatly ironed arses being shoved in their face
The smell of sweat and overcooked plastic
Thickening the air into a nauseating soup;
You can see it run down their cheeks

In the pockmarks
Of the weathered men
In the drooping mascara
Of the teenaged girls.

Somewhere distant a baby cries
The platform announcer calmly apologizes
For delays, those still with places to be
Wriggle to the exit, hammering
The new buttons in ignorant panic

Until granted early release from this
Rolling cage, the train clearing out,
Becoming comprehensible again,
The forty degree day lingering

Until the final stop is reached;
If only I were sitting at the beach.
