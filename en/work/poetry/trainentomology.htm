7am train entomology

Chasing the morning Sun
Down long undeviating paths,
I feel every bump rock
The bone spurs of my arse

The sprain of ever tightening clocks
With interlocking gears, the cords
Wrapped around my neck in the packed
7am train people in pressed suits
And school uniforms squeeze together

Like the beetles in the matchbox
Of a particularly cruel child
And a plane flies by out the window
Holding a steady pace

Against the scurrying insect race
Slowing to a crawl
As the units are collated
And profit calculated

My shoulders ache and
My feet grow tired of waiting
For the chance to walk;

The Sun sits small
Over the distant hills: I feel
I’d rather be an entomologist.
