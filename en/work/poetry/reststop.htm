Rest Stop (On Brand Hwy)

Wedged between the black trunks and orange leaves
Between a burnt out scrubland and a massive artifice of earth,
The red dirt scarred by lines where the tears have fallen,

I take my rest on the great ride north, my feet sink deep
Into the ashen soil, on the road
It is long and lonely

But as I sit and drink my tea
In this desolate place, the noise of all
The passing cars with caravans and fishing boats
Is furious, incessant —

When I come to the north
Why do I always feel mournful within;
The Noongar said it was a land
To the West,

I am out of their country now
And my sorrow is truly boundless
