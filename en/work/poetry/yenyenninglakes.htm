Yenyenning Lakes

Come down to Yenyenning Lakes
The home of the Beverley Ski Club
Where the water is crisp to nonexistent
And tonnes of salt form an endless plain

To project your dreams and lurid fantasies on
As if you were on the beach
In Waikiki sipping a piña colada

But you are here in the Wheatbelt again
Sipping a French tea from a Japanese cup
Made in India and China respectively
On a picnic rug in mud-encrusted shoes

— There was water after all —

Watching the thinnest most dejected looking
Sad smudge of moisture hug the far side of the lake
Like the first hairs on a teenaged boy’s lip

And instead of chiselled surfer boys
And girls not wearing much more than
The leis around their necks

Your company is a couple of retirees
In an RV half a kilometre away
And a brigade of inquisitive ants

Marching over the new terrain of your blanket
And all your things, and into
The long dark tunnels of your pants.

You wish you brought your skis
To this wonderful place
As the ants make claim to your body
And one takes a chunk of your back;

Do you not also
Pinch people when you feel threatened?
Playing with tiny terracotta shells
In the sand, you feel like a child again
